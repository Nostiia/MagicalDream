Comfortable bed

First step to achive magical dreams is to get comfortable and anatomic bed. When you sleep in this kind of beds, you wake up in good mood and good self-feeling.

Here is some tricks to select right bed
  • Look for a mattress that supports your spine's natural alignment without sagging or being too firm.
  • Choose the firmness (soft, medium, or firm) based on your sleeping position:
    • Side sleepers: Softer for shoulder/hip support.
    • Back sleepers: Medium-firm for spinal alignment.
    • Stomach sleepers: Firmer to prevent sinking.
  • If you sleep hot, opt for breathable materials like latex or mattresses with cooling technology.
  • For couples, choose a mattress with good motion isolation (like memory foam) to avoid disturbances.
  • Match your pillow height and firmness with your sleeping position to avoid neck pain.
  • Before buying lie down for at least 10-15 minutes in your typical sleeping position.
  • Choose a bed with a generous trial period (30–100 nights) and warranty (10+ years).

Health

  • Humans spend about one-third of their lives sleeping.
  • REM sleep is essential for dreaming and emotional regulation.
  • Sleep needs decrease with age but never fall below 6 hours.
  • A lack of REM sleep can impair memory and learning.
  • The longest recorded period without sleep is 11 days (not recommended and dangerous).

General Facts

Recommended Sleep Duration

  • Adults (18–64 years): 7–9 hours per night.
  • Teens (14–17 years): 8–10 hours per night.
  • Children (6–13 years): 9–11 hours per night.
  • Infants (4–11 months): 12–15 hours per day.

Sleep Cycles

Sleep consists of 4 stages: 3 non-REM stages and 1 REM stage. A full sleep cycle lasts about 90–120 minutes and repeats 4–6 times a night.

Circadian Rhythm

The body's internal clock regulates sleep and wakefulness. It's influenced by light exposure, meal timing, and other habits.

Health Benefits of Sleep

Brain Function

Enhances memory, learning, and decision-making. Improves problem-solving and creativity.

Immune System

Strengthens the immune response. Sleep deprivation increases susceptibility to illness.

Heart Health

Supports healthy blood pressure levels. Reduces the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Emotional Regulation

Reduces stress, anxiety, and depression. Helps maintain emotional stability.

Metabolism and Weight Management

Regulates appetite-controlling hormones (ghrelin and leptin). Lack of sleep can lead to overeating and weight gain.

Physical Recovery

Muscle repair and growth occur during deep sleep. Promotes faster recovery from injuries.

Sleep Disorders

Insomnia

Difficulty falling or staying asleep.

Sleep Apnea

Breathing stops and starts repeatedly during sleep.

Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)

An uncontrollable urge to move legs, usually in the evening.

Narcolepsy

Sudden episodes of sleep during the day.

Parasomnias

Abnormal behaviors during sleep (e.g., sleepwalking, night terrors).

Lifehacks

Create a Sleep-Conducive Environment

Keep your bedroom dark, quiet, and cool (65°F or 18°C is ideal). Use blackout curtains or an eye mask.

Stick to a Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

Limit Screen Time

Avoid screens (phones, tablets, TVs) at least 1–2 hours before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin production.

Diet and Hydration

Avoid heavy meals, caffeine, and alcohol close to bedtime. Stay hydrated but limit fluid intake in the evening.

Exercise Regularly

Physical activity promotes better sleep, but avoid intense exercise close to bedtime.

Relaxation Techniques

Practice mindfulness, meditation, or deep breathing to reduce stress before bed.

Limit Naps

If needed, limit naps to 20–30 minutes in the early afternoon.
